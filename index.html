Plant Disease Identification using CNN

Our project, "Plant Disease Identification using Convolutional Neural Networks (CNN)," focuses on leveraging AI to detect and classify plant diseases early, ensuring timely intervention and reducing crop losses.

In recent years, agricultural technology has seen significant advancements, aiming to increase efficiency and sustainability. Our project, "Plant Disease Identification using Convolutional Neural Networks (CNN)," focuses on leveraging AI to detect and classify plant diseases early, ensuring timely intervention and reducing crop losses.

We employed CNN, a deep learning algorithm, to analyze images of plant leaves. The dataset consisted of thousands of leaf images, each labeled with specific disease signatures. These images were used to train the CNN model, enabling it to learn and recognize various disease patterns.

The trained model achieved a 95% accuracy rate in identifying common diseases in plants such as powdery mildew, rust, and blight. The system can process images in real-time, providing instant feedback to farmers and agricultural experts.

The application of CNN in plant disease identification demonstrates a scalable solution for early disease detection, potentially saving billions in agricultural losses annually. This technology empowers farmers with precision agriculture tools, promoting sustainable farming practices by reducing unnecessary pesticide use.

Integration of ORB-SLAM with YOLOv8

This project aims to enhance autonomous navigation systems in robotics and vehicles, providing them with advanced spatial awareness and object identification capabilities.

The integration of Simultaneous Localization and Mapping (ORB-SLAM) with You Only Look Once version 8 (YOLOv8) represents a groundbreaking advancement in real-time object tracking and environmental mapping. This project aims to enhance autonomous navigation systems in robotics and vehicles, providing them with advanced spatial awareness and object identification capabilities.

We combined ORB-SLAM, a feature-based 3D SLAM approach, with YOLOv8, a fast real-time object detection system. ORB-SLAM constructs a 3D map of the environment, while YOLOv8 identifies and classifies objects within the scene. The integration was achieved through a custom software layer that processes data from both systems simultaneously.

The integrated system was capable of accurately mapping environments and identifying objects in various conditions, including low-light and cluttered scenes. It demonstrated improved navigation accuracy in autonomous drones and vehicles, especially in complex urban environments.

Integrating ORB-SLAM with YOLOv8 offers significant social value in enhancing autonomous navigation, reducing accidents, and paving the way for fully autonomous transport systems. It also has potential applications in disaster response, where quick and accurate mapping and analysis are critical.
